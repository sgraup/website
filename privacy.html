IFMachine is an Android app for playing interactive-fiction games (Z-machine and Glulx story files) published by {{COMPANY_LEGAL_NAME — match DUNS record}} ("we", "us"). This policy explains, in plain language, what data the app touches and what it does with it.

The short version: IFMachine does not contain analytics, advertising, crash-reporting, or tracking SDKs of any kind. It stores your game progress and settings on your device, optionally syncs your saves to your own Google Drive if you turn that on, and contacts the Interactive Fiction Database (IFDB) only when you explicitly search for a game or pick cover art.

If you want the long version, read on.

1. What we don't do

We do not operate any servers that collect data from IFMachine. We do not transmit any of the following to any server we control:

  • Your name, contact details, phone number, or physical address
  • Your physical or device location (the app requests no location permissions)
  • Your device's advertising ID, IMEI, MAC address, or other hardware identifiers
  • Analytics events, usage telemetry, session data, or interaction logs
  • Crash reports
  • Any data for marketing, profiling, or third-party sharing

The app does not include Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, Google Analytics, AdMob, the Facebook SDK, or any comparable tracking library.

The two situations in which information does leave your device are described in §3 (optional Google Drive sync of save files) and §4 (optional IFDB lookups). Both are off by default and require an explicit action by you. Section 2 lists what the app stores locally on your device — including the Google account name once you connect Drive sync, which is kept on-device so the Settings screen can show you which account is linked.

2. What is stored on your device

IFMachine stores the following on your device only (in the app's private storage area, not accessible to other apps):

  • Game files you import or download (.z3–.z8, .ulx, .zblorb, etc.)
  • Save files and autosaves generated as you play
  • Library metadata — title, author, IFID identifier, import date, and the path to any cover art you selected
  • Cover art images you select (either from Blorb resources embedded in the game file, or from IFDB via the cover-art picker)
  • App settings — theme, font, map style, navigation panel position, etc.
  • A debug log (ifminput.log) — diagnostic markers and unimplemented opcode traces used for development. The log may contain game text and player commands; it never contains personal identifying information. The log stays on your device unless you choose to export it via the Debug tab.
  • An install identifier (UUID) — a random identifier generated once when you first install the app. It is used inside save files to distinguish your devices when resolving cloud sync conflicts. It is not tied to your name, account, or any external identity.
  • Connected Google account name — only if you enable Drive sync in Settings — the Google account name (usually an email address) of the account you signed in with. It is stored locally so the Settings screen can display which account is currently connected. It is never sent to any server we operate; see §3 for how it is used by Google's own Drive authentication flow.

This data lives in the app's private storage and is removed when you uninstall the app.

3. Cloud sync with Google Drive (optional)

If you enable cloud sync in Settings, IFMachine will sync your save files only to a folder of your choice in your own Google Drive. To do this:

  • You pick a Google account and a Drive folder using the standard Android system pickers. We never see your password.
  • The app requests the drive.file OAuth scope, which means it can only read and write files it has created in your Drive — it cannot see your other Drive content.
  • Only save files are uploaded. Your library metadata, preferences, cover art, and debug logs are not uploaded.
  • Your install identifier is embedded inside each save file's metadata chunk to detect conflicts between devices. It stays in the save file if the save is uploaded.
  • The Google account email address you signed in with is shown in IFMachine's Settings screen so you know which account is connected. It is stored locally on your device and sent only to Google's own servers as part of the standard OAuth flow.
  • You can turn sync off, change folders, or sign out of Drive at any time from Settings. Files already uploaded remain in your Drive until you delete them yourself.

We do not run any servers that receive your save data. Saves go directly between your device and your own Google Drive.

4. IFDB integration (optional)

When you search for a game in the Browse screen, use the cover art picker, or download a game from the Interactive Fiction Database (ifdb.org), IFMachine makes HTTPS requests to ifdb.org to fetch search results, game metadata, or cover-art thumbnails. These requests:

  • Are only made when you take an explicit action (search, pick cover art, download a game)
  • Contain only the search terms you typed, or the IFDB identifier (TUID) of a specific game
  • Do not include your name, email, account, device identifier, or any other information about you
  • Carry a generic User-Agent header of IFMachine-Android/0.1 (no device model, Android version, or other identifying info)

Story files you download from IFDB may be hosted on third-party archives (such as the Internet Fiction Archive). Downloading a game causes IFMachine to retrieve the file from that host using a standard HTTPS request.

We do not control ifdb.org or the archives it links to. Their respective privacy policies apply to data they receive (which from IFMachine is limited to the request information listed above).

5. Permissions

IFMachine requests two Android permissions:

PermissionPurpose
INTERNET Cloud sync to your Google Drive (if enabled) and IFDB requests (when you initiate them)
ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE Detect whether you are on Wi-Fi vs. cellular, so cloud sync can respect a Wi-Fi-only preference

We do not request location, camera, microphone, contacts, phone, calendar, or sensor permissions.

6. Children's privacy

IFMachine does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13. The app does not ask for age, does not show advertising, and does not collect any data that would identify a user.

Some interactive-fiction games available through IFDB are written for adult audiences. IFMachine itself does not curate or rate game content. Parents and guardians should supervise downloads of third-party game files as they would any other internet content.

7. Data sharing

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your data with anyone. We don't collect data we could share.

The only third party that ever receives information from your use of IFMachine is:

  • Google, when you enable Drive sync (saves uploaded to your own Drive) or when you sign in to a Google account through the system account picker.
  • IFDB / IFArchive, when you explicitly search or download (request contents listed in §4).

In both cases, the data goes from your device directly to those services; we do not see it.

8. Your choices

  • Stop using cloud sync: turn it off in Settings. Past saves remain in your Drive until you delete them.
  • Delete your data: uninstalling IFMachine removes all locally stored data (saves, settings, library, cover art, logs). To delete cloud-synced saves, delete the synced folder in your Google Drive.
  • Use the app without an account: cloud sync is opt-in. You can use every feature of IFMachine without ever signing in.
